What is a Static QR Code?

A static QR code contains information encoded directly into the pattern. Once generated, the link cannot be changed, making it perfect for technical sheets, product labels, and printed materials that need to last without subscriptions or expiration dates.

Print-Ready Quality

Our generator produces 1000x1000 pixel files. This resolution ensures perfect sharpness on A4 documents, brochures, and packaging. Even when resized to 2cm, the code maintains enough density for any smartphone to read.

How to Generate Vector Formats (SVG/EPS)

For large-scale uses like window decals or 1-meter banners, we recommend using a vector format. Here is how to process our file:

  1. Download the high-resolution PNG file from this site.
  2. Open a graphic design software like Adobe Illustrator or the free Inkscape.
  3. Use the "Image Trace" function set to "Black and White Logo".
  4. Expand the result: you will have a perfect vector QR code that can be scaled infinitely without quality loss.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Do the generated QR codes expire?

No, our QR codes are static. This means the information is encoded directly into the pattern and will never expire. Just ensure your destination URL remains active.

Is there a scan limit?

There are no limits. Since they are static codes, they can be scanned an infinite number of times with no costs or restrictions.

Can I change the link after printing?

No. In static codes, the link is permanent. To change the destination, you would need to generate and print a new code.

What is the minimum size for printing?

We recommend a minimum size of 2x2 cm (0.8x0.8 inches) to ensure all smartphones can read it easily, especially for longer URLs.

Is my data safe?

Absolutely. We do not store your URLs or track scan data. Everything happens locally in your browser.
